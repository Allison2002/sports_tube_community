The Historical Context

Early Beginnings

Women’s participation in hockey can be traced back to the late 19th and early 20th centuries, when women began playing organized hockey in Canada and the United States. However, these early efforts were often met with skepticism and limited support. The lack of formal leagues and opportunities hindered the growth of women’s hockey, leaving many talented players without a platform to showcase their skills.

The Formation of Leagues

The establishment of the first women’s hockey leagues in the late 1990s and early 2000s marked a turning point for the sport. The National Women's Hockey League (NWHL) was formed in 2015, providing a professional platform for female athletes to compete at the highest level. This was followed by the creation of the Premier Hockey Federation (PHF), which has continued to elevate the profile of women’s hockey.
