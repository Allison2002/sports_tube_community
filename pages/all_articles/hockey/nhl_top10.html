Top 10 Unforgettable Moments
in NHL History

by Genny Allison | AI-Generated Content by Allison Stock Inc | March 15, 2025

The National Hockey League (NHL) is home to some of the most exhilarating moments in sports history. From game-winning goals to unforgettable Stanley Cup celebrations, these moments showcase the passion, drama, and skill that make hockey so thrilling. In this article, we countdown the top 10 NHL moments of all time, highlighting the iconic plays and performances that have captured the hearts of fans around the world.

  1. The "Miracle on Ice" (1980)

    The "Miracle on Ice" is one of the most iconic moments in sports history. During the 1980 Winter Olympics in Lake Placid, the underdog U.S. team, composed mostly of amateur players, faced off against the heavily favored Soviet Union. In a shocking upset, the U.S. won 4-3, igniting a nationwide passion for hockey and forever cementing the event’s place in history.

  2. Bobby Orr’s Flying Goal (1970)

    One of hockey's most memorable images is Bobby Orr flying through the air after scoring the game-winning goal in overtime during the 1970 Stanley Cup Finals. His leap as he scores against the St. Louis Blues is still etched in the minds of fans and symbolizes the pure joy of victory. That goal not only sealed the championship for the Boston Bruins but also established Orr as one of the greatest players of all time.

  3. The 1972 Summit Series

    The 1972 Summit Series between Canada and the Soviet Union is widely regarded as one of the greatest series in hockey history. This eight-game series brought together the best players from both nations, with memorable moments from stars like Bobby Clarke and Vladislav Tretiak. The series concluded with a dramatic goal by Paul Henderson in the final minutes of Game 8, making it a defining moment in the sport’s international rivalry.

  4. Mario Lemieux’s Return (2000)

    After battling cancer, Mario Lemieux made an inspiring return to the NHL on December 27, 2000, playing for the Pittsburgh Penguins. In his first game back, Lemieux scored a goal and assisted on two others, showing that his talent and determination were undiminished. His comeback became a symbol of resilience and hope, inspiring fans and players alike.

  5. The Gretzky Trade (1988)

    Wayne Gretzky’s trade from the Edmonton Oilers to the Los Angeles Kings in 1988 shocked the hockey world. Known as "The Trade of the Century," this move didn’t just affect the fortunes of both teams—it also helped popularize hockey in the U.S., elevating the league’s profile and attracting new fans, especially in Southern California.

  6. The 2010 Stanley Cup Finals: Blackhawks End the Drought

    After a 49-year championship drought, the Chicago Blackhawks secured the Stanley Cup in 2010, marking a new era for the franchise. In a dramatic Game 6, Patrick Kane scored the game-winning goal in overtime, igniting a celebration at the United Center and bringing the cup back to Chicago for the first time in nearly half a century.

  7. Dominik Hasek’s "Save" (1999)

    Dominik Hasek’s miraculous save against Eric Lindros of the Philadelphia Flyers during the 1999 playoffs is often cited as one of the greatest saves in hockey history. With an incredible display of athleticism, Hasek robbed Lindros of a sure goal, solidifying his reputation as one of the game’s most dominant goaltenders.

  8. Sidney Crosby’s Golden Goal (2010)

    Sidney Crosby’s overtime goal in the 2010 Winter Olympics secured the gold medal for Canada in a thrilling final against the United States. Crosby’s clutch goal not only highlighted his exceptional skill but also cemented his legacy as one of hockey’s greatest players, making him a national hero in Canada.

  9. The Miracle at MSG (1994)

    The New York Rangers’ 1994 Stanley Cup victory ended a 54-year championship drought. Game 7 of the Finals against the Vancouver Canucks was filled with drama, but the Rangers rallied from behind to win 3-2. The victory sparked an explosion of joy in New York City, as fans celebrated the long-awaited win.

  10. Alex Ovechkin Lifts the Cup (2018)

    After years of playoff heartbreak, Alex Ovechkin and the Washington Capitals finally clinched the Stanley Cup in 2018. Ovechkin’s emotional celebration as he lifted the Cup above his head became one of the most iconic images in recent memory, marking the culmination of a career dedicated to greatness and passion for the game.

Conclusion

These top 10 moments in NHL history are more than just plays—they represent the heart, passion, and spirit of hockey. From unforgettable goals to historic victories, these moments have defined the sport and continue to inspire fans and players. As the NHL evolves, we eagerly await the next moment that will cement itself in hockey history.
