Simone Biles: Redefining Gymnastics Simone Biles has forever changed the landscape of gymnastics, both as a competitor and as a voice for mental health in sports. As a four-time Olympic gold medalist, 25 World Championship medals, and numerous national titles later, Biles' achievements are not just about the number of medals she has won, but the way she has redefined the sport itself. Her unprecedented performances, including the introduction of the “Biles” moves—the most difficult skills ever performed by a gymnast—have set new standards in gymnastics that will likely stand for decades. But beyond her athletic ability, Biles' decision to prioritize her mental health during the Tokyo 2020 Olympics sent a powerful message that resonates with athletes and fans alike. During the competition, Biles pulled out of several events, citing her struggles with the "twisties," a psychological phenomenon that affects gymnasts’ ability to maintain control over their bodies during aerial routines. This bold decision to step away from competition, rather than push through and risk injury, sparked a global conversation about mental health in sports, an issue that has long been overlooked. Biles' vulnerability has helped remove the stigma surrounding mental health in athletics, and her story has inspired countless athletes to speak out about their own struggles. In doing so, she has contributed to a cultural shift in the way mental well-being is prioritized in sports. Her actions highlight the importance of self-care, not just physical strength, and remind us that true strength comes from knowing when to take a step back for your health. In addition to her advocacy, Biles continues to be a trailblazer for young gymnasts, particularly young girls of color, showing them that they too can achieve greatness in a field that has historically been dominated by white athletes. Her continued leadership in and out of the gym has solidified her place not only as a gymnastics legend but also as a powerful advocate for the future of athletes’ mental health.
