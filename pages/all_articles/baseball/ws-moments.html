Top 10 Most Memorable
World Series Moments

by Genny Allison | AI-Generated Content by Allison Stock Inc | March 15, 2025

The World Series, the pinnacle of Major League Baseball, has provided fans with countless unforgettable moments throughout its storied history. From game-winning home runs to breathtaking pitching performances, these moments have defined the championship and captivated audiences across generations. Here, we countdown the top 10 most memorable World Series moments that have left an indelible mark on the game.

  1. Babe Ruth's Called Shot (1932)

    In Game 3 of the 1932 World Series, Babe Ruth famously pointed to the center field stands before hitting a home run. This iconic moment occurred during a tense matchup between the New York Yankees and the Chicago Cubs at Wrigley Field. Ruth's dramatic gesture, whether he actually called his shot or not, has sparked debates among baseball historians for decades. The home run solidified Ruth's larger-than-life persona, showcasing his confidence and flair. It was a defining moment that not only elevated Ruth’s legacy but also left an unforgettable imprint on baseball lore.

  2. Don Larsen's Perfect Game (1956)

    Don Larsen etched his name in history when he pitched the only perfect game in World Series history during Game 5 of the 1956 series against the Brooklyn Dodgers. The game took place on October 8, and Larsen's performance was nothing short of miraculous, as he faced 27 batters and retired them all. The tension mounted with each out, and the final play, a ground ball to shortstop, sent fans into a frenzy. Larsen’s achievement not only stunned fans but also highlighted the pressure of the championship stage, making it a monumental feat in baseball history.

  3. Bill Mazeroski's Walk-Off Home Run (1960)

    In Game 7 of the 1960 World Series, Bill Mazeroski hit a walk-off home run in the bottom of the ninth inning to give the Pittsburgh Pirates a stunning victory over the New York Yankees. This moment is especially memorable because it was the first time a World Series was decided by a walk-off home run in Game 7. The ball soared over the left-field fence at Forbes Field, and as Mazeroski rounded the bases, the crowd erupted in joy. This historic home run not only secured the Pirates’ championship but also remains a cherished memory for fans, highlighting the excitement and unpredictability of baseball.

  4. Carlton Fisk's Game 6 Home Run (1975)

    Carlton Fisk's iconic home run in Game 6 of the 1975 World Series is forever etched in the minds of baseball fans. In the 12th inning against the Cincinnati Reds, Fisk hit a towering shot that hit the foul pole. As the ball soared, Fisk famously waved his arms to urge the ball to stay fair, capturing the intensity of the moment. The ensuing celebration was electrifying, as Fisk's emotional reaction and the dramatic nature of the game made it one of the most memorable moments in baseball history. The game ultimately went down as one of the greatest in World Series history, culminating in a dramatic victory for the Boston Red Sox.

  5. The 1986 Buckner Incident

    In Game 6 of the 1986 World Series, the Boston Red Sox were just one out away from their first championship in 68 years when a ground ball rolled through first baseman Bill Buckner's legs. This unexpected error allowed the New York Mets to tie the game, and they ultimately won in extra innings. The moment is both painful and unforgettable, showcasing the drama and unpredictability of sports. Buckner’s misplay became a symbol of the Red Sox’s struggles, leading to a long history of “the curse.” The incident serves as a reminder of how quickly fortunes can change in baseball.

  6. Kirk Gibson's Walk-Off Home Run (1988)

    Kirk Gibson, hobbled by injuries, made a stunning pinch-hit appearance in Game 1 of the 1988 World Series. With two outs in the bottom of the ninth, he faced Oakland Athletics closer Dennis Eckersley and hit a dramatic walk-off home run. Gibson’s triumphant fist pump as he rounded the bases became a symbol of resilience and determination. The moment exemplified the unpredictability of baseball, proving that even when the odds are stacked against you, magic can happen. This moment remains one of the most iconic in World Series history, showcasing the power of the human spirit.

  7. The 1991 World Series: "The Greatest Ever"

    The 1991 World Series between the Minnesota Twins and Atlanta Braves is often hailed as one of the greatest ever played. Game 7, in particular, featured a masterful pitching duel between Jack Morris and John Smoltz. Morris pitched 10 scoreless innings, leading to a dramatic 1-0 victory for the Twins in 10 innings. The suspense and excitement of this game are forever etched in baseball history, as it showcased two teams battling for the title in a game that had fans on the edge of their seats. The combination of stellar pitching and late-game drama solidified this series' place in baseball lore.

  8. The Curse of the Bambino Broken (2004)

    After an 86-year championship drought, the Boston Red Sox finally broke the "Curse of the Bambino" in the 2004 World Series. The Red Sox swept the St. Louis Cardinals, culminating in a moment of jubilation as they celebrated their long-awaited victory. The victory parade that followed in Boston was filled with ecstatic fans, and the emotions ran high as the team lifted the trophy. This championship not only marked a significant moment for Red Sox fans but also transformed the franchise's legacy in the sport, erasing decades of disappointment and heartache.

  9. Madison Bumgarner's Dominance (2014)

    In the 2014 World Series, San Francisco Giants pitcher Madison Bumgarner delivered one of the most remarkable performances in postseason history. He pitched five scoreless innings in Game 7 against the Kansas City Royals, securing the championship for the Giants. Bumgarner’s incredible ability to perform under pressure and his complete-game shutout earned him the World Series MVP award. His performance not only exemplified dominance on the mound but also showcased the impact a single player can have on the outcome of a championship, solidifying his place in baseball history.

  10. The Chicago Cubs End Their 108-Year Drought (2016)

    The Chicago Cubs' victory in the 2016 World Series marked the end of a 108-year championship drought. The Cubs defeated the Cleveland Indians in a thrilling Game 7, which featured a rain delay and extra innings. The emotional celebrations that followed, including fans' tears of joy, symbolized a historic moment in baseball history and solidified the Cubs' place in the hearts of their fans. This victory was not just a win for the team; it was a collective sigh of relief for generations of Cubs supporters who had waited their entire lives to see their team win the championship.

These ten moments capture the essence of what makes the World Series so special—its ability to evoke emotion, create legends, and unite fans across generations. Whether it's a historic home run, a perfect game, or a dramatic comeback, the World Series continues to be a stage where unforgettable memories are made, and baseball's rich history is celebrated. As we look forward to future championships, these moments remind us of the enduring legacy of America's pastime.
