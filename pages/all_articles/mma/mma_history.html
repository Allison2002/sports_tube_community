Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) has become one of the most electrifying and globally recognized combat sports in the world. 1. Ken Shamrock vs. Royce Gracie – UFC 1 (1993)

The very first UFC event set the stage for what would become a global phenomenon. At UFC 1, Royce Gracie, a Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu specialist, faced off against Ken Shamrock, a seasoned wrestler and submission artist. This fight was not only the first-ever UFC battle but also a moment that demonstrated the power of grappling in a mixed martial arts setting. Gracie's victory by rear-naked choke against Shamrock highlighted the effectiveness of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu in real combat, leading to a paradigm shift in training for MMA athletes. The event marked the beginning of the evolution of MMA, with BJJ becoming a cornerstone of successful fight strategies.

2. Forrest Griffin vs. Stephan Bonnar – The Ultimate Fighter Finale (2005)

This fight is often credited with saving the UFC and bringing MMA into the mainstream. Forrest Griffin and Stephan Bonnar went toe-to-toe in a bloody, back-and-forth battle that captivated millions of fans watching on television. The fight, which aired as part of the first season finale of *The Ultimate Fighter*, was a brutal showcase of heart and determination. Griffin won by unanimous decision, but it was the sheer intensity and excitement that helped the UFC grow exponentially. This match is regarded as one of the most important moments in UFC history, drawing in a wave of new fans and setting the stage for future UFC success.

3. Anderson Silva vs. Chael Sonnen – UFC 117 (2010)

Few fights are as memorable as the 2010 showdown between Anderson Silva and Chael Sonnen at UFC 117. Sonnen dominated Silva for almost four rounds, controlling the pace and dictating the fight with superior wrestling. However, Silva’s striking brilliance and unwavering composure shone through in the fifth round. In a jaw-dropping turn of events, Silva submitted Sonnen with a triangle armbar, capping off one of the most thrilling comebacks in MMA history. This victory not only preserved Silva's undefeated streak but also solidified his place as one of the greatest middleweights of all time.

Ronda Rousey was a dominant force in women’s MMA, with an undefeated record and several title defenses. However, at UFC 193, Holly Holm delivered a shocking knockout that stunned the MMA world. With a perfectly timed head kick, Holm dethroned Rousey in the second round, handing the reigning champion her first loss in an unforgettable moment. This fight marked the rise of Holm to superstardom and signaled the arrival of new competition in women's MMA. 5. Conor McGregor vs. José Aldo – UFC 194 (2015)

The rivalry between Conor McGregor and José Aldo reached its peak at UFC 194, where McGregor challenged Aldo for the UFC Featherweight Championship. After months of heated trash talk and mind games, McGregor delivered on his promise. In just 13 seconds, McGregor knocked Aldo out with a left hook, clinching the featherweight title and becoming the first fighter in UFC history to hold two belts simultaneously. The fight was a defining moment in McGregor’s career, cementing his place as one of the most polarizing and charismatic figures in combat sports.

At UFC 229, Khabib defended his lightweight title against McGregor in a fight that not only had sports implications but also personal and cultural ones. After dominating McGregor for most of the fight, Khabib secured a submission victory in the fourth round. However, the real drama unfolded after the fight when Khabib leapt over the cage and charged toward McGregor’s cornermen, sparking a brawl. 7. Amanda Nunes vs. Cris Cyborg – UFC 232 (2018)

Amanda Nunes faced Cris Cyborg in a monumental battle of two of the greatest female fighters in MMA history. At UFC 232, Nunes shocked the world by defeating Cyborg in the first round via knockout, claiming the UFC Featherweight Championship. Nunes became the first female fighter in UFC history to hold two championships in different weight classes simultaneously. This victory solidified Nunes's status as one of the most dominant forces in women's MMA.
